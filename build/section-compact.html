Definition 5.1

A collection \(\mc A\subseteq \mc P(X)\) is said to cover a subset \(Y\subseteq X\) iff \(Y\subseteq\bigcup\mc A\text{.}\)

Definition 5.2

A subset \(K\subseteq X\) of a topological space is said to be compact iff for every collection of open sets \(\mc U\) covering \(K\text{,}\) there exists a finite subcollection \(\mc F\subseteq\mc U\) that also covers \(K\text{.}\)

Example 5.3

Determine if each of the following subsets of the Euclidean line is compact.

  1. \(\mb R\)
  2. \(\mb Z\)
  3. \(\setBuilder{2^{-n}}{n\in\mb N}\)
  4. \(\setList{0}\cup\setBuilder{2^{-n}}{n\in\mb N}\)
  5. \((0,1)\)
  6. \([0,1]\)
Definition 5.4

A subset \(R\subseteq X\) of a topological space is said to be relatively compact iff for every collection of open sets \(\mc U\) covering \(X\text{,}\) there exists a finite subcollection \(\mc F\subseteq\mc U\) that covers \(K\text{.}\)

Theorem 5.5

A space is regular if and only if for every point \(x\) and neighborhood \(U\text{,}\) there exists a neighborhood \(V\) of \(x\) such that \(x\in V\subseteq\cl V\subseteq U\text{.}\)

Theorem 5.6

Let \(X\) be regular. A subset \(R\subseteq X\) is relatively compact if and only if \(\cl R\) is compact.

Theorem 5.7

Let \(X\) be compact and \(K\) be a closed subset of \(X\text{.}\) Then \(K\) is compact.

Proposition 5.8

Every finite subset of a space is compact.

Proposition 5.9

Every finite union of compact subsets is compact.

Theorem 5.10

Every compact subset of a Hausdorff space is closed.

Proposition 5.11

Let \(\mc T=\setList{\emptyset}\cup \setBuilder{\mb N\setminus F}{F\text{ is finite}}\) be the cofinite topology on \(\mb N\text{.}\) Every subset of \(\mb N\) is compact under this topology.

Theorem 5.12

Let \(f:X\to Y\) be continuous and \(K\subseteq X\) be compact. Then \(f[K]\) is compact.

Corollary 5.13

Compactness is a topological property.

Theorem 5.14

Every infinite subset of a compact set has a limit point.

Theorem 5.15

Let \(\mc K=\setBuilder{K_n}{n\in\mb N}\) be a collection of non-empty compact subsets of a topological space such that \(K_{n+1}\subseteq K_n\) for all \(n\in\mb N\text{.}\) Then \(\bigcap\mc K\) is a non-empty compact set.

Theorem 5.16

Let \(X\) be metrizable. Then the following are equivalent for \(K\subseteq X\text{.}\)

  1. \(K\) is compact
  2. Every infinite subset of \(K\) has a limit point.
  3. Every infinite subset of \(K\) has a sequential limit point.
Lemma 5.17

A topological space \(X\) is Hausdorff if and only if for every pair of disjoint compact subsets \(H,K\) there exist disjoint open sets \(U,V\) such that \(H\subseteq U\) and \(K\subseteq V\text{.}\)

Theorem 5.18

Every compact Hausdorff space is \(T_4\text{.}\)
