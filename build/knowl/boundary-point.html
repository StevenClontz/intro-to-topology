Definition 2.32

Let \(S\subseteq X\) be a subset of a topological space. The point \(x\) is a boundary point of the set \(S\) if and only if for every neighborhood of \(U\) of \(x\text{,}\) both \(S\cap U\) and \(S\setminus U\) are non-empty.

Let \(\bd S\) collect all the boundary points of \(S\text{.}\)
