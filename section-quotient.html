Section 8 Quotient Spaces

¶
Definition 8.1

A quotient map is a surjection \(f:X\to Y\) such that \(V\subseteq Y\) is open if and only if \(f^\leftarrow[V]\subseteq X\) is open.

Proposition 8.2

Let \(\tuple{X,\mc T_X}\) be a topological space, and let \(f:X\to Y\) be a surjection. Then \(\mc T_Y=\setBuilder{V\subseteq Y}{f^\leftarrow[V]\in\mc T_X}\) is a topology on \(Y\) such that \(f\) is a quotient map.

Definition 8.3

The topology defined in Proposition 8.2 is known as the quotient topology induced by \(f\text{.}\)

Definition 8.4

Let \(X^*\) be a partition of a topological space \(X\text{,}\) and let \(f:X\to X^*\) be the surjection given by letting \(f(x)=A\) iff \(x\in A\text{.}\) Then \(X^*\) paired with the quotient topology induced by \(f\) is called a quotient space or identification space.

Theorem 8.5

Let \((X\times Y)^*=\setBuilder{\setList{x}\times Y}{x\in X}\) partition the product \(X\times Y\text{.}\) Then the quotient space \((X\times Y)^*\) is homeomorphic to \(X\text{.}\)

Definition 8.6

A subset \(R\subseteq X^2\) is called a relation on \(X\text{,}\) where the notation \(xRy\) is equivalent to writing \(\tuple{x,y}\in R\text{.}\)

A relation \(\sim\) on \(X\) is called an equivalence relation if it satisfies the following for all \(x,y,z\in X\text{.}\)

  1. \(x\sim x\text{.}\) (Reflexivity)
  2. \(x\sim y\) implies \(y\sim x\text{.}\) (Symmetry)
  3. \(x\sim y\) and \(y\sim z\) implies \(y\sim z\text{.}\) (Transitivity)
Theorem 8.7

Let \(X^*\) be a partition of \(X\) and define the relation \(\sim\) on \(X\) such that \(x\sim y\) if and only if \(\setList{x,y}\subseteq A\) for some \(A\in X^*\text{.}\) Then \(\sim\) is an equivalence relation.

Theorem 8.8

Let \(\sim\) be an equivalence relation on \(X\text{,}\) and let \([x]=\setBuilder{y\in X}{x\sim y}\text{.}\) Then \(X^*=\setBuilder{[x]}{x\in X}\) is a partition of \(X\text{.}\)

Definition 8.9

Let \(\sim\) be an equivalence relation on a topological space \(X\text{.}\) Then \(X/\sim\) denotes the quotient space defined by the partition \(X^*\) given in Theorem 8.8.

Proposition 8.10

Let \(R\) be a relation on \(X\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \sim=\bigcap\setBuilder{\sim^\star\subseteq X^2}{R\subseteq\sim^\star\text{ and }\sim^\star \text{ is an equivalence relation on }X} \end{equation*}

is an equivalence relation on \(X\text{.}\)

(Therefore, an equivalence relation may be defined as the minimal equivalence relation satisfying a list of relationships.)

Example 8.11 Curves and surfaces defined as quotients

Show that each of the following Euclidean subspaces and quotients of Euclidean subspaces are homeomorphic.

  1. \([0,1]/\sim\) where \(0\sim 1\text{,}\) and \(\setBuilder{\tuple{x,y}\in\mb R^2}{x^2+y^2=1}\text{.}\)
  2. \([0,2]/\sim\) where \(0\sim 1\sim 2\text{,}\) and \(\setBuilder{\tuple{x,y}\in\mb R^2}{(x-1)^2+y^2=1}\cup\setBuilder{\tuple{x,y}\in\mb R^2}{(x+1)^2+y^2=1}\text{.}\)
  3. \([0,1]^2/\sim\) where \(\tuple{0,y}\sim\tuple{1,y}\text{,}\) and \(\setBuilder{\tuple{x,y}\in\mb R^2}{1\leq x^2+y^2\leq 2}\text{.}\)
  4. \([0,1]^2/\sim\) where \(\tuple{x,y}\sim\tuple{z,w}\) whenever at least one of \(x,y\) and at least one of \(z,w\) is in \(\setList{0,1}\text{,}\) and \(\setBuilder{\tuple{x,y,z}\in\mb R^2}{1\leq x^2+y^2+z^2=1}\text{.}\)
Definition 8.12

The hypersphere of dimension \(n\) is the quotient space \(S^n=[0,1]^n/\sim\) given by \(\vec x\sim\vec y\) whenever there exist \(i,j\in\setList{0,\dots,n}\) such that \(\vec x(i),\vec y(j)\in\setList{0,1}\text{.}\)

Definition 8.13

The Möbius strip is the quotient space \(M=[0,1]^2/\sim\) given by \(\tuple{0,y}\sim\tuple{1,1-y}\text{.}\)

Definition 8.14

The torus is the quotient space \(T=[0,1]^2/\sim\) given by \(\tuple{0,y}\sim\tuple{1,y}\) and \(\tuple{x,0}\sim\tuple{x,1}\text{.}\)

Definition 8.15

The Klein bottle is the quotient space \(K=[0,1]^2/\sim\) given by \(\tuple{0,y}\sim\tuple{1,1-y}\) and \(\tuple{x,0}\sim\tuple{x,1}\text{.}\)
